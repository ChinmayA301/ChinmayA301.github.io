## Context

Local governments increasingly use AI-assisted workflows for service routing, fraud detection, and resident communication. Public adoption depends on clear guardrails, measurable fairness, and transparent explanations of automated outcomes.

## Framework for AI Impact Auditing

### 1. Service Journey Mapping

Map each resident touchpoint where AI influences the outcome:

- Intake and triage
- Eligibility or prioritization decisions
- Follow-up and escalation

### 2. Segmentation Transparency

Document what segmentation variables are used, why they are used, and what outcomes they influence. Keep a plain-language explanation for public-facing materials.

### 3. Equity and Performance Monitoring

Track quality and fairness metrics by segment:

- Resolution rate
- Time-to-service
- Error and override rate
- Complaint frequency

## Implementation Pattern

A practical implementation can combine:

- Decision logs from AI-enabled systems
- Human override records
- Resident feedback channels
- Monthly governance review with remediation actions

## Why This Improves Digital Experience

When residents understand how AI affects service delivery and see evidence of active auditing, trust improves. Better segmentation documentation also helps teams reduce inconsistent handling across programs.

## Conclusion

AI in local government should be managed as a public accountability system, not just a technical deployment. Auditable segmentation and transparent communication are central to improving trust in digital services.
